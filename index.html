III. Human and Behavioral Risks: The Core of Prevention

The human factor is the most decisive component. Addressing it is the core of effective and sustainable prevention.

Highlights as an example.

1. Normalization and Low Perception of Risk

The most dangerous precursor to serious incidents. It occurs when shortcuts are accepted and signals are ignored due to complacency, familiarity, and leadership influence, among others.

Ex: On a forklift, lifting a person on the forks, instead of using a personnel lift with a safety cage. The forklift must be operated by authorized and trained personnel. In some countries, it is forbidden for plant personnel who do not meet this requirement to operate a lift.

2. Unsafe Behavior and Attitude

The "it won't happen to me" attitude and distractions (cell phone use) promote unsafe acts. Deep behavioral training is needed.

Data: 6% decline in active observation of safe behavior.

3. Psychosocial Factors and Mental Health

Priority issue identified by the working group. Mental health is closely related to safe work performance.

Direct impact on attention, decision-making, and behavior.

IV. Challenges and Opportunities

Keys to building a proactive and sustainable safety culture.

Integration of AI and efficient use of advanced technologies

Use AI for predictive risk analysis and training.

New Generations

Adapt communication and learning formats to the styles of younger workers.

Behavioral Culture

A fundamental challenge is the need to change the organizations' mindset beyond mere regulatory compliance, towards a culture where health and safety are Basic Human Values of the business. Where the health and safety culture is aligned with the organizational culture and therefore with the country's culture.

Visible Leadership

Promote visible, committed, and consistent leadership that leads by example.

Active Participation

Involve all people in risk identification and proposing continuous improvements.

Intergenerational Strategy

Leverage the experience of veterans and the innovative perspective of youth, working together to "learn how to learn."

Planning and Analysis

Reinforce planning, risk assessment, and situational analysis before each task.

Contractor Inclusion and Management

Given the growing trend of outsourcing, it is imperative to ensure contractor adherence to all safety strategies, establishing clear contractual requirements and classifying them for focused risk management.

Comprehensive management of the human factor and attitude

It is a challenge to measure and align individual psycho-emotional competencies with the demands of the task, recognizing that factors like impulsivity, attention to detail, or stress management influence safety.
